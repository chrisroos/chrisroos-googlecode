  Some things I learnt at "interesting 2009":http://russelldavies.typepad.com/planning/interesting2009/.  Note.  Some of this stuff is probably made up.  If you want to see what actually happened you should read the "coverage by Roo Reynolds":http://rooreynolds.com/2009/09/12/interesting-2009-forty-even-more-interesting-things/ (in theory my numbers match the numbers in Roo's list).

  # Boats can fly.
  # The kids of today will feel sorry for us because we don't take steroids.  THEN THEY WILL DESTROY US.
  # We are all gentlemen.
  # Cheating in computer games is good, as long as you don't get caught.
  # Adventure books can be funny.
  # Stan Laurel's dad invented theatre.
  # God invented subduction zones for us to hide our nuclear waste in: they're like giant dustcarts.
  # Robots are all made of win (although not clear, I believe this is some advanced alien technology).
  # Twitter can blow bubbles.
  # Lilac doesn't exist.
  # Keyboards are string instruments (ok, so this is actually true but I had to think about it when it was being talked about, because I'm an idiot).
  # r2d2 has been made into every product ever built.  Apollo 22 was actually made to look like the little fella.
  # Morse invented kazoos and chocolate.
  # Maps are better when they're a list of words, rather than, you know, a map.
  # Much loved girl comics from the 70s and 80s were written by famous horror writers, including Stephen King.
  # Francis Galton could smell maths.
  # Children's art is better than a lot of what's in the Tate Modern.
  # Magners isn't cider.
  # Origami is actually impossible and all origami seen on TV has been computer generated by a supercomputer running for weeks on end.
  # It's easier to buy strands of banana smell dna and upload them into yoghurt than it is to buy a banana flavoured yoghurt.
  # Conductors love coffee, chocolate cake and david beckham.
  # You can never record too much data about your daily life.
  # Breast shot is also the name of a waterwheel.
  # Cartoons are like films that play at about 1 frame per second for 6 frames.
  # India has superpowers.
  # Burn giant wooden men or kill goats.  It's up to you.
  # Writing a book takes ages so make sure you pick a fairly general topic (social web apps, or religion, I guess).
  # Having imaginary ponies doesn't stop you from going on stage and talking about said imaginary ponies.
  # Cycling around the UK takes you up someones wife's lane.
  # Bill and Ted, the musical, is being presented live in and around Oxford next year.
  # Darwin invented Chinese Whispers.
  # Making incomprehensible noise.  There's an app for that.
  # All train lines are built on top of underground rivers.
  # You shouldn't play Monopoly.
  # Some schools have now completely reversed the role of pupils and teachers with the pupils now teaching the staff.
  # John Prescott listens to his ipod at night while planting sunflowers.
  # Astrotagging is done by a little robot that comes out of your computer and sits on your desk.
  # 1. Make clothes, 2. Make TED clone, 3. You're done.
  # France has the only bullet viewing platform, on top of the Eiffel tower.
  # Sunsets are caused by the sun disappearing.

  All in all it was a great, and inspiring, day.
